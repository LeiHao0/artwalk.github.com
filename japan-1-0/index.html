Kyoto

After returning from Italy during the eleven-day holiday,
possibly because the credit card was maxed out, China Merchants Bank sent a premium card.

Half a month later, LittleMaster’s childhood friend was going to Japan to see autumn leaves,
and had already planned the itinerary:
Kyoto -> Nara -> Kinosaki -> Osaka
Unfortunately, it was difficult to take time off work, so only tickets for early November were booked, hoping to catch the end of the autumn leaves season.

When LittleMaster saw that it was an area in Kansai that he had never been to,
he immediately indicated that he had obtained a three-year multiple entry visa in June
and could go together.

Although there were only two weeks left before departure, time was tight,
luckily managed to get a flight ticket, worked hard for two weeks, and finished the tasks ahead of schedule.
After submitting the code to the organization, the second trip to Japan 🇯🇵 began.

At the time of buying the ticket, I didn’t know what kind of plane it would be.
When I arrived at the airport and saw this small and thin airplane, everyone was stunned.
It turned out to be a Boeing 737.
Although the MAX incident hadn’t happened at that time,
I still worried that the plane wouldn’t be able to fly and would crash into the sea,
turning instead to the Lost storyline.


Kyoto Subway Station

Last time in Tokyo, I waited at the same platform for different subways.

It was the first time LittleMaster encountered such a huge shopping mall as a subway station,
with a total of 7 floors, including dining, entertainment, and everything.
Although it was only November, the Christmas atmosphere was already in the air.

Arashiyama Scenic Train

Early the next morning, went to take the scenic train in Arashiyama, which was said to have picturesque views.
However, one must buy a ticket on the side close to the mountain, or else one can only hear other tourists exclaiming “wow” while not being able to see anything themselves 😂
