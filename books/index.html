📖 Libraries

🇸🇬 Library@harbourfront

Seaside library with lounge chairs
You can stare at the cable car and the small train going back and forth to Sentosa 🚠🚄
Then go to Vivo City for delicious food 😋

🇸🇬 Library@orchard


This building is confusing, I’ve been there several times and still can’t find the entrance

🇸🇬 The LLibrary


I used to come here a lot before, it’s only 5 minutes away from my house, I would take the bus straight after work at 6:30
They have these magical cylindrical seats 💺, designed specifically for people with autism
They update their collection frequently, including the latest releases 📖
There are floor-to-ceiling windows, and power outlets 🔌, so you can charge your laptop 💻 and they provide Wi-Fi

It’s my spot - Sheldon Cooper

I would curl up there until the library closed at 10:30 pm 🕥
It feels like I’m back to the days when I used to study in Hangzhou

🇺🇸 Cupertino Library



They provide plenty of study spaces
They have USB ports and charging plugs
I found the book “Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?” and “2001: A Space Odyssey” there

🇦🇺 State Library of Victoria


This is also a whole building, the interior is like a maze

🇦🇺 State Library of Queensland


Many students 👨‍🎓👩‍🎓 working in groups on their assignments
There are small lizards and raccoons outside 🦝??

🇨🇳 Nanjing, Hangzhou, Beijing




Nanjing, Shanghai Library

You need a card to enter

West Lake District Library

This is probably the library I visit most often, even though it’s not big, it has a lot of good books, like “The Wisdom of Crowds” and “Thinking, Fast and Slow”
Since my office and apartment are nearby, I would go there after work and study until closing time
Because they have air conditioning and hot water, I would bring Soylent with me on weekends, so I don’t have to go out for lunch and worry about losing my spot
It was also there that I accidentally came across Lin Da’s “I Have a Dream”
I looked up the series at the time, and every book title amazed me, not to mention the content of the books
Later on, I kept fantasizing about one day being able to go to the US and experience it for myself, to see if it’s as wonderful as described in the books

Hangzhou Library

Located in Civic Center, it’s huge, the bookshelves are so high that you have to use ladders like in Harry Potter to reach them
It’s great, but it’s in Binjiang District, so it’s too far, I’ve only been there a few times

National Library

The National Library is also very big, but it’s always crowded, and security checks are strict, you can only bring an iPad with you, I’ve only been there once
